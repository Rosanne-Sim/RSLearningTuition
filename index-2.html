About Rosanne

A tutor who explains the "why", not just the answer.

For the past three years, I've worked one on one with primary and secondary students across Singapore, helping them build real skills in English and Math rather than memorise answers for the next test. I started tutoring because I kept seeing capable students fall behind simply because no one had ever explained things in a way that made sense to them.

Having gone through A-Levels myself, I understand exactly what your child is up against. I'm now completing a Data Science and Business Analytics degree at the University of London, which keeps my own analytical thinking sharp.

Every student I take on begins with an honest assessment. I find out what's solid, what's shaky, and what's never actually been taught properly, then I build a plan around that specific child. Clear explanations, real practice, and steady feedback, so you always know exactly where your child stands and what I'm working on with them next.

Whether the goal is catching up, staying ahead, or preparing for PSLE or O-Levels, I bring the same patience and attention to every session. My priority isn't just the grade on the report card. It's making sure your child understands the subject well enough to carry it forward on their own.

Teaching Approach

How every lesson is actually taught

MOE-Aligned Materials

Every lesson is built around the current MOE syllabus, using the latest past year papers from schools. I vary the difficulty deliberately, so your child is stretched at the right pace rather than overwhelmed or under-challenged.

Drawn straight from real past year papers.

Guided Questioning

Instead of handing over the answer, I ask the questions that lead your child there themselves. It takes longer in the moment, but it's how a child moves from just getting the mark to actually thinking independently, and eventually, critically, on their own.

This is the part that actually changes how a child thinks.

Diagnosing the Root Cause

When a mistake keeps happening, I look past the wrong answer to find out why. Often it's a specific misunderstanding, or a habit of rushing and guessing rather than working through it properly. Once I know the real cause, I can help your child shift it, not just paper over it.

The mistake stops being a pattern.

Questions

A few things parents usually ask

Do you teach online, in person, or both?

In person only, at the student's home. I've found this works best for focus and consistency.

How long is each lesson?

1.5 to 2 hours, depending on what works best for you and your child.

What if my child needs to reschedule?

Just let me know at least 24 hours in advance and we'll figure out another slot together.

Do you follow the MOE syllabus?

Yes. Lessons are planned around your child's current school syllabus and topics, with extra practice material where it's needed most.

How do you keep me updated on my child's progress?

I always share an update after the first lesson, then check in monthly after that, over WhatsApp or in person if you're home at the time. If you'd like an update sooner, just ask and I'll fill you in.

Do you give homework between sessions?

Usually not. Most students are already stretched with school work, and my sessions are paced with enough rigour that extra homework is rarely necessary. If a student would benefit from more practice, I'll say so directly.

Do lessons continue during the school holidays?

Yes, lessons carry on as usual. If you'd like longer or more frequent sessions during the holidays, just let me know and I'll accommodate where my schedule allows.

Can my child message you with questions outside lesson time?

Yes, within reasonable hours. I'm happy to help clarify something between sessions rather than have a student stay stuck on it until the next lesson.

Do you teach siblings or multiple children together?

Yes, I'm happy to teach siblings or a small group of children under the same roof.

What if my child isn't improving after a few months?

I'll speak with you and adjust the approach. If we've tried a genuinely different method and there's still no progress, I'll be upfront with you and suggest another tutor who may be a better fit for your child.
